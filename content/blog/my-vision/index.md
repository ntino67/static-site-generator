# My vision on life

This blog post will lay my philosophy like an open book, I'll try to be as clear as possible because this is a pretty interesting mental exercise.

---

## My philosophy

The best way to describe my philosophy and the way I see it is: "Shyness is a cage, get out of it by doing small brave things repeatedly and the side-effect is a more interesting life and happier people around you".

That's it, that's my motto. Of course, all of it should be moderate, I won't go out in the streets and yell horrible things. I just want to be happy and I believe that a lot of where it is coming from is by making others happy.

I try to make others happy as much as possible within what's possible. If I see someone catching my eye in the street, I'll genuinely compliment them and leave promptly. If I see a guy with a jacket that looks cool, I'll tell him. If I see someone in need of help unpacking their groceries, I'll be here. If I see an elderly person on a bench, I'll ask them their story.

The fact that I'm a stranger for them and that I'm genuinely trying to compliment or help them never fails to make them smile. And that makes me happy. If someone complimented me out of the blue without asking anything in return, I can't help but believe that it's genuine. And IT'S genuine.

This is what I call small brave things. I was originally a very shy guy but, getting out there and talking to people, learn their story, what makes them THEM is so interesting and it allows me to see lives with a lot of different perspectives. And most of all, allows me to have a life that's way more exciting! I have so many tales to tell to everyone and will tell many stories to my children.

### Those brave little things

Now, you're maybe asking yourself: "But Matteo, I can't do those brave things, this is too much of a step-up compared to me now!", and I'll tell you that you're right. Bravery is subjective but it shouldn't be recklessness. The keyword in this section title is little. This is where you need to think.

You may not be comfortable with talking to strangers, so don't talk to strangers for now. Go out of your comfort zone, but enough so that you can continue without fearing it.

Start by offering a coffee to a colleague, be genuinely interested in his life and hearty in your praise. Then ask the ice-cream vendor for recommandations, try to argue with them on why vanilla is overrated anyway and compliment them on their hard-work. Then ask random people the time, that's it no more. Smile at them, try to search connection. Then, try to search some excuses to go talk to them. And finally, talk to whoever you want, whenever you want, that's how I did it.

You will be uncomfortable and that's normal, but that's a small price to pay compared to the rewards.

## Conclusion

Life is so much fun when you actually allow yourself to do whatever you want. I believe that this is the core of my philosophy. I want to do that, I'll do it, no excuses. I want to go out with this girl, I'll shoot my shot. I want to try dance, I'll try it. I want to be good at coding, I'll be good. Life is already hard as it is, don't make it harder. Allow yourself to live as yourself and not whatever the society or anybody that isn't you said you are.

You are already a miracle by being alive so live.
